📡 How to Read Your Sensor Data
1
💧
Soil Moisture
Normal: 40–65%
▼

Soil moisture tells you how much water is in the ground. Think of it like a sponge — you want it between 40–65% full for most crops.

  • !Below 35% — Very dry. Irrigate immediately. Roots can't absorb nutrients without water.
  • ✓35–65% — Healthy range. Monitor but no action needed.
  • !Above 75% — Too wet. Stop irrigation. Risk of root rot and fungal disease.

The range bar on each sensor card shows a green zone (normal) and a moving dot showing the current value. If the dot is outside the green zone, the reading needs your attention.

2
🧪
Soil pH
Normal: 6.0–7.0 pH
▼

pH measures how acidic or alkaline your soil is. pH 7 is neutral; lower = more acidic, higher = more alkaline. Most crops grow best between 6.0 and 7.0.

  • !Below 6.0 (acidic) — Apply agricultural lime (calcite or dolomite). Acidic soil locks out phosphorus, calcium, and magnesium.
  • ✓6.0–7.0 — Ideal for most crops. No action needed.
  • !Above 7.5 (alkaline) — Apply sulphur or ammonium sulphate. Alkaline soil blocks iron and manganese.
3
🔥
Smoke / Air Quality
Normal: 0–15 ppm
▼

Smoke sensors detect fine particles in the air. Normal air is 0–15 ppm. Readings above 50 ppm indicate nearby burning or fire.

  • ✓0–15 ppm — Clean air, no concern.
  • !15–50 ppm — Mild smoke. Check for burning in adjacent fields or livestock areas.
  • !Above 50 ppm — Active smoke. Dispatch inspection immediately. Do not send workers into the area without visual confirmation.
4
🌿
Grass Density (Pastures)
Normal: 50–100%
▼

For pasture zones, grass density shows what percentage of the ground has healthy grass cover. Below 50% means the pasture is being overgrazed.

  • !Below 40% — Move cattle out immediately. The land needs at least 3 weeks of rest to recover.
  • ✓50–100% — Healthy pasture. Animals have enough to eat.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions
💡 What does the percentage next to "Health Score" mean?▼
The Health Score (0–100) is calculated from the soil moisture reading of that zone. A score below 35 = Critical (red), 35–60 = Warning (amber), above 60 = Healthy (green). For livestock pastures, it reflects the water tank level instead.
📡 Why are some sensors showing "WATCH" when the value looks normal?▼
WATCH means the value is currently within range but trending in a bad direction — for example, soil moisture is at 38% but has been dropping for 3 days. The AI flags it early so you can act before it becomes a CRITICAL alert.
🌾 How often does the AI update the Plan of Action?▼
The Plan is regenerated every time sensor data is synced (every 3 seconds in the live simulation). In a real deployment with IoT sensors, it would update every 15–30 minutes. The plan always reflects the current state of your farm.
➕ How do I add a new zone to my farm?▼
Go to the Zones page. Click the "⊕ Add Zone" button in the zone list on the left. Fill in the zone name, crop type, area (hectares), water source, and soil data, then click "Add Zone". The new zone appears on the map immediately.
📱 Does AgroSense work on mobile phones?▼
Yes. The app is fully responsive. On mobile, use the bottom navigation bar (Home, Zones, Sensors, Alerts, Plan, Guide). The hamburger menu (☰) in the top-left opens the full sidebar. All pages are optimized for phone screens.
🔔 How are alerts sent to my team?▼
Critical alerts are sent via Telegram bot (already connected) and SMS if enabled in Settings → Notifications. You can also configure email digests and push notifications. Go to Settings → Notifications to manage these preferences.
🌐 How do I change the language?▼
On desktop: use the KZ / RU / EN language switcher in the top navigation bar. On mobile: open the sidebar using the ☰ menu button, and the language switcher is accessible there. Switching language instantly translates all labels, titles, and navigation items.
